What's the matter?

  • Most quantum mechanical systems, even in one dimension, are not soluble by analytical means. Developing a quick numerical and general method is desired (some good ones already exist in the market).
  • A systematic way to compute observables non-perturbatively is desired. (Some already exist, but they have limitations)
A couple of personal believes:
  • Wavefunction is just an artifact. Maybe less controversial: observables should be computable without any reference to a wavefunction.
  • Quantum Mechanics is a probabilistic theory. Should be described by probabilistic quantities


  • An a philosophy: Physical observables should be highly constraint just by physical conditions. Not fancy math required. (path integrals, variational methods, etc...). This philosophy is known as the bootstrap.



Bootstrap in Quantum Mechanics



General Method

Any physical observable ${\cal O}$ which is an eigenstate of the Hamiltonian, must satisfy the following conditions (Xizhi Han, Sean A. Hartnoll, Jorrit Kruthoff, 2020): $$\langle [H,{\cal O}]\rangle=0\,,$$ $$\langle H{\cal O}\rangle =E\langle {\cal O}\rangle\,,$$ $$\langle {\cal O}^{\dagger}{\cal O}\rangle\geq 0\,,$$ The End.

Explicit Method

Let's take a generic 1D Hamiltonian, $$H={p^2\over 2M}+V(x)\,,$$ We want to apply the fist two conditions above $\langle [H,{\cal O}]\rangle=0\,,$ $\langle H{\cal O}\rangle =E\langle {\cal O}\rangle\,.$

There are two alternative ways to approach this,

  • Pick a few convenient "generic" operators ( for a polynomial $V(x)$, ${\cal O}=x^s$ and ${\cal O}=x^m\,p$), and use recursively the fundamental commutation relation $[x,p]=i\hbar.\quad$($\hbar$ = 1 in this talk). Perform some commutator gymnastics

  • Write down the Schr√∂dinger equation (I know I said we dont need the wave function, but that is ilustrated in alternative above), multiplied it by $x^s$ and perform integration by parts to get rip of the $p$ operator. This has been developed by Moq and myself and is a generalization of and old result for the Hydrogen atom known and Kramer's relation. More systematic, not need to perform any commutator gymnastics, incorporates boundary condition explicitly.


Either of this approaches, lead us to a recursion relation: $$2m E\langle x^{m-1}\rangle+{m(m-1)(m-2)\over 4}\langle x^{m-3}\rangle-\langle x^{m}V'(x)\rangle-\langle x^{m-1}V(x)\rangle=0\,, $$ which allow us to get higher order monomials from lower order monomials.

A Search for Positivity

Now it comes the important piece in the bootstrap program, as in life :




Take a general operator to be $${\cal O}=\sum_nc_n x^n\,,$$ then for $c_n\in \mathbb{C}$.

By the third condition, it needs to satisfy $$ 0\leq \langle {\cal O}^{\dagger}{\cal O}\rangle=\sum_{i\,,j}c_i^{*}\langle x^{i+j}\rangle c_j\,,$$ or in other words, the matrix $M_{ij}=\langle x^{i+j}\rangle$ has to be positive-definite.

This can be easily implemented on a computer:

  • Use recursion relation to reduce the order of monomials $\langle x^{i+j}\rangle$ in terms of an independent basis, say for the sake of explanation say such basis is $\{\langle x^{0}\rangle,\langle x^{2}\rangle,\, E\}=\{\langle x^{2}\rangle,\, E\}$.
  • Define a large grid (array) for the space $\{\langle x^{2}\rangle,\, E\}$.

  • Define Array M $M_{ij}=\langle x^{i+j}\rangle$

  • Write array in the basis.

  • For $p,\, q$ in grid {
        Evaluate M
          If M$\geq 0\,\,$ {         Save $(p,q)$       else continue } }

We have implemented this in different languages python/fortran/c++ (in performance order) and have been able to compute the energy spectrum of several bounded (and unbounded) 1D quantum systems.

Simple example, quartic anharmonic oscillator, $$H=p^2+w^2 x^2+g x^4$$






Non-Perturbative Physics

Since this is a non-perturbative method, we can use it to capture some non-perturbative phenomena. Take $$H=p^2-|w^2| x^2+g x^4$$


The ground state of these system can be computed in the large frequency regime ($|w^2|\to \infty$), at leading order: $$E_0=-{|w^4|\over 4}+\sqrt{2|w^2|}-{1\over |w^2|}\,. $$ Two-fold degenerate: two-harmonic oscilators plus anharmonic corrections.

It is well known that non-perturbative instanton solutions, split this degeneracy $$\Delta E_0\sim |w|^{5/2}\,e^{-\sqrt{2}|w|^3\over 3}\,.$$


Some potential applications want to consider:
  • Decay of metastable states.
  • Particle production / Schwinger effect (?).
  • Growth order for coefficients in perturbation theory.
