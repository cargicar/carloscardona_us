Welcome

A little bit about my life and background

I was born and raised in the picturesque landscapes of Colombia. Similar to many countries in the Americas, Colombia boasts a population that is highly diverse, representing a wide array of races and ethnicities. In fact, one of the most remarkable aspects of the country is its wide diversity, evident not only in the genetic heritage of its inhabitants but also in its extensive biodiversity. Colombia is ranked as the second most biodiverse nation on Earth, surpassed only by Brazil, which, to be fair, encompasses eight times more land area than Colombia in square kilometers.

I did my undergrad in Physics at Universidad del Valle, Cali-Colombia and received my PhD in Physics from Universidad de Buenos Aires - Argentina. Later I took research positions at highly ranked universities in Brazil, Taiwan, Denmark and the United States. Currently, I am a Visiting Assistant Professor at Case Western Reserve University in Cleveland, OH, USA.

Research

My primary area of research falls within the expansive realm of Quantum Field Theory. I have made significant contributions to Conformal Field Theory, Scattering Amplitudes, and Gravity.

Presently, my primary research focus revolves around exploring the foundational aspects of Deep Learning, examining it through the perspective of complex physical analogical systems.
In particular, much like Physics leverages symmetries to simplify models for complex systems, I contend that Deep Learning should seek analogous symmetries. These symmetries, I believe, hold the potential to help us overcome the pervasive curse of dimensionality that plagues deep learning models.
