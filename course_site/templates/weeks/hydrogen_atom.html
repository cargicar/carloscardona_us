Hydrogen atom is composed by a light negative charged particle (electron) orbiting a heavy (2000 heavier) positively charged particle (nucleus). we can think of the electron as moving around the proton under the influence of the central potential produced by the proton, \begin{equation} u(r)=-{1\over4\pi \epsilon_0}{e^2\over r}\, \end{equation} which looks like


which looks like a deep well. We can expect some general features observed from the infinite well: like energy quantization and stationary solutions. Nevertheless we need to solve the Schrodinger equation for the given potential rigorously. Since $u=u(r)$ it would be unconvenient to use cartesian coordinates, and we want the Schrodinger equation in Spherical coordinates,





The point $(r,\theta,\phi)=(A,\pi/2, \pi/2)$ corresponds to

  1. $(x,y,z)= (A,0,0)$

  2. $(x,y,z)=(0,A,0)$

  3. $(x,y,z)=(0,0,A) $

  4. $(x,y,z)=(0,0,0)$


In spherical coordinates, \begin{equation} \nabla^2={1\over r^2}\left[{\partial\over \partial r}\left(r^2{\partial\over \partial r}\right)+{1\over {\rm sin}(\theta)}{\partial\over \partial\theta}\left({\rm sin}(\theta){\partial\over\partial\theta}\right) +{1\over {\rm sin}^2(\theta)}{\partial^2\over \partial\phi^2}\right] \end{equation} In order to compute probabilities, we will also need the Integration measure, \begin{equation} dV=r^2 \,{\rm sin\theta}\,d\theta\,\phi\, dr \end{equation} 3D Schrodinger equation takes the form, \begin{equation} -{\hbar^2\over 2 m}{1\over r^2}\left[{\partial\over \partial r}\left(r^2{\partial\over \partial r}\right)+{1\over {\rm sin}(\theta)}{\partial\over \partial\theta}\left({\rm sin}(\theta){\partial\over\partial\theta}\right) +{1\over {\rm sin}^2(\theta)}{\partial^2\over \partial\phi^2}\right]\psi(r,\theta,\phi)+U(r)\psi(r,\theta,\phi)=E\psi(r,\theta,\phi) \end{equation} In order to solve this large equation, we will separation of variables method, \begin{equation} \psi(r,\theta,\phi)=R(r)\Theta(\theta)\Phi(\phi)\,, \end{equation} which lead us to the following independent differential equations, $$-{\partial\over \partial r}\left(r^2{\partial\over \partial r}\right)R(r)-{2mr^2\over \hbar^2}(E-U(r))R(r)=C\,R(r)$$
$$ {\rm sin}(\theta){\partial\over \partial\theta}\left({\rm sin}(\theta){\partial\over\partial\theta}\right)\Theta(\theta) -C\,{\rm sin}^2(\theta)=D\Theta(\theta)$$
$$ {\partial^2\Phi(\phi)\over \partial\phi^2}=-D\Phi(\phi)$$
Can you give me a solution of the last one?


The combine solutions $\Theta_{\ell, m_{\ell}}\Phi_{m_{\ell}}$ are known as the spherical harmonics

with $m=-\ell, (-\ell+1),\cdots,0,\cdots,(\ell-1),\ell $.

The Radial equation

$$-{\hbar^2\over 2 m}{1\over r^2}{\partial\over \partial r}\left(r^2{\partial\over \partial r}\right)R(r)+{ \hbar^2 \ell(\ell+1)\over 2mr^2}R(r)+U(r)R(r)=E\,R(r)$$ $$ E_{\rm kinetic}\qquad+\qquad \left(E_{\rm rotational}={L^2\over 2m}\right)+\quad E_{\rm potential}=E_{\rm total} $$ with, \begin{equation} E=-{m e^2\over2 (4\pi\epsilon_0)^2 n^2}={-13.6 eV\over n^2} \end{equation} $$ \boxed{|L|=\hbar\sqrt{\ell(\ell+1)},\quad \ell=0,1,2,\cdots,n-1}\,. $$ The restriction on $\ell$ comes from the fact that rotational energy can't be larger than the total energy. The quantum number $n$ is known as the principal quantum number.
Summarizing, the solutions are, \begin{equation} \psi(r,\theta,\phi)_{n,\ell,m}=R_{n,\ell}(r)\Theta_{\ell,m}(\theta)\Phi_{\ell,m}(\phi)\,, \end{equation} with $$n\in\mathbb{Z}$$ $$\ell\in\mathbb{Z},\quad {\rm and}\quad 0\leq\ell\leq n-1$$ $$-\ell\leq m\leq\ell $$
