As we approach a critical point in a phase diagram, correlation lengths diverge, implying scale-invariance. Such scale invariance is believe to get enhanced to Conformal Invariance.


Conformal Field Theory (CFT) and Quantum Field Theory (QFT)

Its been argued that well-defined QFTs can be viewed as points along an RG Flow from an UV CFT. We can remove the UV cutoff from a QFT (send it to infinite energy) if it can be interpreted as an RG flow from the vicinity of a CFT fixed point.

A mayor question in HET is what is the "lanscape" of possible QFTs?. CFT are landmark points connecting them.

Studying the space of allowed CFTs basically amounts to studying the space of all well-defined QFTs.

The Conformal Bootstrap

How we study the space of CFTs?: One way is by implementing the bootstrap philosophy. Observables are highly constrained by physical requirements, therefore finding a systematic way of imposing such requirements will carve-out the space of physically allowed theories. In the case of CFT: conformal invariance, crossing symmetry, unitarity, operator product expansion.


El-Showk, Paulos, Poland, Rychkov, Simmons-Duffin, Vichi 2012

Bootstrap in Quantum Mechanics

We can even implement the bootstrap in Quantum Mechanics. Recently, in collaboration with undergrad student Almuqtada Al-Yasiri we have made an implementation in fortran/c++/python that is able to compute the energy spectrum of bounded 1D quantum systems.


In collaboration with Prof. Kurt Hinterbichler we have started to think on how to apply this methodology on higher dimensional quantum mechanical systems.

Loss Landscape in Over-Parameterized Deep Neural Networks

Another landscape that is becoming increasingly important, is related to the loss function in Over-Parameterized Deep Neural Networks. In such systems we want to look for THE minimun that generalize best. The main method used to find such local minima, is Gradient Descent (GD) and Stochastic Gradient descent (SGD) and variants. Despite the affordability of (GD), the fact that it has not knowledge of the curvature introduces instabilities.

In collaboration with Michael Hinczewski we have been studying the implications of this instabilities during training, and we have discovered that they produce a repelling force some around local minima.


Late dynamics under GD

By introducing noise, or by doing SGD, the repelling force is screened by the noise. These explain why SGD descent choice more generalizable minimums, which are associated to flatter minima.
