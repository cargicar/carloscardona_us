All materials emit electromagnetic radiation, because they contain charged particles that jiggle around, and an accelerating charge radiates electromagnetic energy. This particular radiation is termed Thermal Radiation.

What other radiation can a body "emit"?

Well, they can reflect radiation, much as a mirror reflect visible radiation.

An object from which electromagnetic radiation emanates solely due to the thermal motion of its charges is known as a Blackbody. Any radiation that strikes it must be absorbed rather than reflected, hence the name.

An experiment is setup such us we can measure the energy emitted by a blackbody (like the sun). In practice the spectral density$={du\over df}$ is measured:


If the electromagnetic radiation behaves strictly as sinusoidally oscillating waves, the spectral density turns out to be $${dU\over df}=k_b T\times {8\pi V\over c^3}f^2\,. $$ This curve is also shown in the plot above, the black curve shows that as the frequency on the radiated wave increases, the spectral energy density grows (quadratically), leading to a divergence in the energy spectral density: The ultraviolet catastrophe".

In a desperate attempt to avoid to solve the such catastrophe, Max Planck suggested that the energy at frequency $f$ is somehow restricted to be proportional to an integer times the frequency $$E=n\,h\,f\,,$$ with $h$ some constant, which leads to following theoretical prediction,
$$\boxed{{dU\over df}={8\pi~ V~h~f^3/c^3\over e^{h f/k_B T}-1} }\,,$$ and which perfectly matches the curve seen experimentally. The value of the constant that adjust the experimental data to the curve above is, $h= 6.63\times 10^{-34} J\cdot s $, the following table is useful,


Planks constant is yet another nature's fundamental constants, as $c$ or the gravitational constant $G$. For it discovery and the resolution of the ultraviolet catastrophe, Max Plank was awarded the 1918 Nobel Prize.

We are coming back to this formula when we cover quantum statistical mechanics.

Discussion

Planck hypothesis $$E=n\,h\,f $$ have deep consequences on our understanding of nature's behavior: it implies that energy can take discrete values, or as we said in physics, is quantized . It seems to imply that electromagnetic radiation behaves as an having an integer number of particles each of energy $hf$. However, even Planck hesitated at the time to make such huge conceptual leap.

Plank's spectral density is a crucial link between temperature and electromagnetic radiation, that we will explore later in the course, but for now, let us look into some practicality of the spectral energy density formula.
