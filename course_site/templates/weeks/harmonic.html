Quantum Harmonic Oscillator

Harmonic Oscillator is a bounded system:
0;256;0c
Therefore, we only need to look into the time-independent Schrodinger equation, \begin{equation} \boxed{ -{\hbar^2\over 2m} {d^2\psi(x)\over dx^2}+u(x)\psi(x)=E\psi(x)}\,. \end{equation} with $u(x)=\frac{1}{2}m\omega^2$.

HO Solutions

Solutions are given by, \begin{equation} \psi_n(x)={\cal N}_n\, e^{-\frac{m\omega x^2}{2\hbar}}H_n\left(\sqrt{\frac{m\omega}{\hbar}}\right),\quad n=0,1,2,\cdots. \end{equation} with, \begin{equation} H_n(z)=(-1)^n\,e^{z^2}\frac{d^n}{dz^n}\left(e^{-z^2}\right)\,,\quad{\rm and}\quad {\cal N}_n=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2^n n!}}\left(\frac{m\omega}{\pi\hbar}\right)^{1/4} \end{equation} First few Hermite polynomial: \begin{equation} H_0(z)=1,\quad H_1(z)=2z,\quad H_2(z)=4z^2-2,\quad \end{equation}


with corresponding energies given by, \begin{equation} E_n=\left(n+\frac{1}{2}\right)\hbar \omega\,.\quad n=0,1,2,\cdots. \end{equation} Notice that in this case $n$ can equal zero.
